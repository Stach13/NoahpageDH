Comparison of the Silmarillion and the Lord of the Rings

Though the diction and style used in the Silmarillion and Unfinished Tales are different than the Lord of the Rings, they all tell stories of adventures. In the N-gram chart you can see that difference between how many times “the sons of feanor” is used versus all the rest.

In this method, I adjusted the ngram count to 3 instead of 4 with "the sons of feanor" statement. This displays twice the frequency using "the sons of", which leads a lot of others that there were either a lot more than one group of "the sons of" or there was another "the sons of" who's tale is told just as frequent as the "sons of feanor".
